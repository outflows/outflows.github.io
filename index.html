About

My main scientific interests are related to astrophysical black holes. In particular, I am working to improve our understanding of the relation between accretion flows - matter that is being pulled towards black holes, and relativistic jets - matter that gets pushed away from the black hole at very high speeds. My goals are to understand the underlying physics behind such extreme environments, and to connect these principles to the observations of different phenomena in a variety of scenarios involving active galactic nuclei and stellar-mass black holes. Check my research for more details.

I am also engaged in outreach activities, since I believe in the importance of promoting awareness and communicating science findings with the general public.
