About

I study accretion flows onto black holes and their relation with relativistic jets. The main tools I use are large-scale computer simulations and Fermi-LAT gamma-ray observations. My goals are to understand the underlying physics behind such extreme environments, especially the mechanisms accounting for the multiwavelength emission in black hole scenarios. Ultimately, I aim to connect these principles to the observations of different phenomena in a variety of scenarios involving active galactic nuclei and stellar-mass black holes. Check my research for more details.

I am also engaged in outreach activities, since I believe in the importance of promoting awareness and communicating science findings with the general public.
